2. 法庭意见书要素

Case Background

James Halbman, a minor, purchased a 1968 Oldsmobile from Michael Lemke for $1,250. The contract was made on July 13, 1973, with Halbman paying $1,000 initially and agreeing to pay $25 per week until the balance was paid.

Key Issues

  • Whether a minor must make restitution for damage to property prior to disaffirmance
  • The scope of the infancy doctrine protection
  • Balance between minor protection and vendor rights

Court's Holding

The court held that a minor who disaffirms a contract for the purchase of an item which is not a necessity may recover his purchase price without liability for use, depreciation, damage, or other diminution in value, absent misrepresentation or tortious damage to the property.

Rationale

The infancy doctrine is designed to protect minors from improvident contracts. Requiring restitution for depreciation would defeat this purposeThe court emphasized that such a requirement would force minors to bear the cost of the very improvidence from which the infancy doctrine is supposed to protect them. by binding the minor to obligations the law allows them to avoid.
