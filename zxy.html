2. Court Opinion Elements

Case Citation

Brown v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483 (1954)

Key Facts

Racial segregation in public schools

Legal Issue

Does segregation violate Equal Protection?

Holding

Separation is inherently unequal

Rationale

Psychological harm to minority children

Precedent Overruled

Plessy v. Ferguson (1896)

Landmark Decision Analysis

The Supreme Court's unanimous decision in Brown v. Board of Education marked a pivotal moment in American constitutional law, establishing that racial segregation in public education violates the Equal Protection Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment.

典型句型 Sentence Patterns

"Segregation of children in public schools solely on the basis of race deprives the children of the minority group of equal educational opportunities."
"仅仅基于种族的公立学校儿童隔离剥夺了少数族裔儿童平等的教育机会。"
"Separate educational facilities are inherently unequal."
"分离的教育设施固有地是不平等的。"
"The doctrine of 'separate but equal' has no place in the field of public education."
"'隔离但平等'的原则在公共教育领域没有立足之地。"
"We conclude that in the field of public education the doctrine of 'separate but equal' has no place."
"我们得出结论，在公共教育领域，'隔离但平等'的原则没有立足之地。"
"To separate them from others of similar age and qualifications solely because of their race generates a feeling of inferiority."
"仅仅因为他们的种族而将同龄且资质相似的其他人分开，会产生自卑感。"
