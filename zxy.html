2. Court Opinion Elements

Case Citation

Brown v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483 (1954)

Key Facts

Racial segregation in public schools

Legal Issue

Does segregation violate Equal Protection?

Holding

Separation is inherently unequal

Rationale

Psychological harm to minority children

Precedent Overruled

Plessy v. Ferguson (1896)

Landmark Decision Analysis

The Supreme Court's unanimous decision in Brown v. Board of Education marked a pivotal moment in American constitutional law, establishing that racial segregation in public education violates the Equal Protection Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment.
