Case Name & Citation

Brown v. Board of Education of Topeka, Shawnee County, Kan.

347 U.S. 483 (1954)

Procedural History

Consolidated appeals from US District Courts in Kansas, South Carolina, Virginia, and Delaware

Facts

Black children denied admission to public schools attended by white children under laws requiring racial segregation

Legal Issue

Does racial segregation in public schools violate the Equal Protection Clause of the 14th Amendment?

Holding

Segregation in public education is unconstitutional as it inherently denies equal educational opportunities

Reasoning

"Separate educational facilities are inherently unequal" - overturned Plessy v. Ferguson's "separate but equal" doctrine

Key Quote

"Separate educational facilities are inherently unequal"

Disposition

Cases remanded for further argument on implementation of desegregation decrees
